Scenario Background

Your organization runs a Flask-based e-commerce platform on AWS EC2. The application has a product price lookup feature. A developer introduced an SSRF vulnerability by using user-supplied input in requests.get() without validation.

SOC has detected unusual outbound traffic from your production web server. You need to investigate and respond.

Exercise Injects

SSRF-DETECT-001 T+0 Initial Detection high

Your SOC monitoring fires an alert: Unusual outbound HTTP traffic to IP 169.254.169.254. 247 requests from production web server. Customer reports unauthorized password resets.

Discussion

  • What is 169.254.169.254 and why is traffic to it suspicious?
  • What is our first action?
  • Who should be on the incident response team?
SSRF-IMPACT-001 T+20 Impact Assessment critical

Forensics confirm credentials stolen. Threat intel reports credentials on dark web. 50GB customer data downloaded from S3. This is a confirmed data breach.

Discussion

  • What is our blast radius?
  • Who needs to be notified internally?
  • What are our regulatory notification requirements?
SSRF-CONTAIN-001 T+40 Containment critical

Attacker has created an IAM user and Lambda function for persistence. They've also modified a legitimate Lambda to exfiltrate data.

Discussion

  • How do we kick the attacker out while preserving evidence?
  • What other persistence mechanisms might they have established?
SSRF-RECOVER-001 T+65 Recovery high

The attacker has been evicted. Credentials rotated. Now planning safe return to operations with enhanced monitoring.

Discussion

  • How do we safely return to operations?
  • What monitoring prevents recurrence?
  • What do we tell affected customers?
